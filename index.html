What I Actually Believe

Takes law firm owners push back on.

These aren't hot takes for content. They're positions I've formed across hundreds of engagements.

Ownership ≠ expertise.

Your equity stake and your name on the door don't make you the smartest person in the room. Get out of the weeds and let the professionals you hired do their jobs.

Growth without freedom is just a bigger job.

Most law firm owners don't actually want growth — they want freedom. Revenue and headcount that add stress aren't success.

AI will replace the billable hour. Not eventually — now.

Every admin and low-level associate task is repetition with a different client. That's a vending machine replacing the soda pop guy, and most firms aren't ready for it.

Most consultants are anecdotes.

One firm sold for a decent exit doesn't make someone a law firm operations expert. Breadth across hundreds of engagements is a track record. One is just a story.

Big platforms are overrated. Small builds are underrated.

Most firms don't need a $400/month SaaS product. They need a targeted automation that solves a specific problem — built by someone who knows what the problem actually is.

The right answer is always firm-specific.

If the same recommendation would work at any firm, it's not a recommendation — it's a template. Everything is tailored to the actual firm, or it's not worth giving.

Your firm doesn't need another report. It needs an operator.

Let's run a diagnostic and find out what's actually holding you back.
